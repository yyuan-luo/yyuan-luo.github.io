Leveraging Semantic 3D City Models for Enhanced Radar Object Detection via Self-Supervised Radar-Image Learning

Abstract

Semantic 3D city models in the format of CityGML are an extensive and easily accessible source of prior environmental information with the potential to mitigate the influence of noise in radar data on tasks such as radar object detection. However, to our knowledge, no prior studies have explored this topic. This paper proposes an autoencoder-based model developed under the contrastive \gls{ssl} paradigm that leverages semantic 3D city models to enhance radar object detection. The model's encoder is trained contrastively against images in the pretext task and subsequently transferred to detect objects with the decoder. We evaluate our model extensively on our independently collected dataset and demonstrate significant improvements over baseline models.
